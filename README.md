# Cryogenic Air Separation through Fractional Distillation

## Overview

This project explores the industrial process of separating air into its primary components—oxygen, nitrogen, and argon—using cryogenic air separation techniques. The study focuses on process flow, thermodynamic principles, and operational aspects of Air Separation Units (ASU).

## Objectives

* Understand the working of cryogenic air separation systems
* Analyze the role of compression, cooling, and purification
* Study fractional distillation for separation of air components
* Evaluate operating conditions and process efficiency

## Process Description

The air separation process involves multiple stages:

1. **Air Compression**
   Atmospheric air is compressed to high pressure for further processing.

2. **Cooling & Purification**
   Air is cooled and impurities like moisture, CO₂, and hydrocarbons are removed.

3. **Cryogenic Cooling**
   The purified air is cooled to very low temperatures (~ -180°C) to liquefy it.

4. **Fractional Distillation**
   Liquid air is separated in distillation columns based on boiling points:

   * Nitrogen (-196°C)
   * Argon (-186°C)
   * Oxygen (-183°C)

## Key Learnings

* Understood thermodynamic principles behind cryogenic processes
* Analyzed the importance of pressure and temperature control in ASU
* Studied the design and operation of distillation columns
* Gained insight into industrial-scale gas separation techniques

## Applications

* Oxygen supply in hospitals and steel industries
* Nitrogen for inert atmospheres in chemical processes
* Argon for welding and electronics manufacturing
